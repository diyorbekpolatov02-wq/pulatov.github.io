Welcome!

I am an astronomer and early-career researcher specializing in solar physics and space weather, with a particular focus on Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs), Interplanetary Coronal Mass Ejections (ICMEs), solar radio bursts, Forbush decreases, and cosmic ray modulation. I am currently a Laboratory Assistant in the Department of Nuclear Physics and Astronomy at Samarkand State University, where I also completed my MSc in Astronomy (2023–2025).

My research investigates the compositional signatures of ICMEs across solar activity cycles, their relationship with geomagnetic storms, and the impact of solar activity on space weather. My MSc thesis, titled *Study of Compositional Signatures of Interplanetary Coronal Mass Ejections (ICMEs) during Solar Activity Cycles*, was supervised at Samarkand State University named after Sh. Rashidov.

I participated in the **COSPAR Capacity Building Workshop** (Samarkand, Uzbekistan, 2024) and the **São Paulo School of Advanced Science on Solar Activity and Space Weather** (Brazil, 2024). In 2025, I received a **COSPAR Capacity Building Workshop Fellowship** at the Udaipur Solar Observatory, Physical Research Laboratory, India, working under the supervision of Professor Nandita Srivastava on the relationship between CME/ICME and their impact on space weather.

I am the author and co-author of several peer-reviewed publications and conference proceedings, covering topics such as solar flare classification, Type II radio bursts, Forbush decreases, and ICME compositional properties. I also participated in international collaborations including the Indo-Uzbek joint research project (Indo-Uzbek-2021-95) on space weather consequences of CMEs and the COSPAR2024 CALLISTO Uzbekistan radio burst study.

My technical skills include Python (AstroPy, SunPy, NumPy, Matplotlib), IDL, OriginLab, and data visualization tools. I am a Winner of 2nd Place at the Republican Science Olympiad in Physics (2023) and an active participant in international scientific forums.
